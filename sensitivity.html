ONE-AT-A-TIME SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

Which calibration constant drives the forecast?

Every forecast rests on a set of estimated parameters. A committee member who trusts the model asks one question first: which of those parameters would, if slightly wrong, change the answer the most? This tornado chart answers that by varying each calibration constant one at a time by ±20%, holding all others at their calibrated values, and recording the shift in projected end-of-life year.

What this tells us

Method

Each parameter is perturbed independently (one-at-a-time, OAT). The remaining parameters are held at their calibrated values. The forward sediment-accounting loop from Engine 6 is re-run in JavaScript for each variant, using the same equations that power the interactive Simulator. The loop runs forward from 2024 until storage crosses the 5.3 MCM dead-storage threshold, and the year of that crossing is reported as the variant's EOL.

OAT analysis is the standard first-line sensitivity method. It identifies dominant parameters cheaply and transparently. A full global sensitivity analysis (Sobol indices, Morris screening) would additionally quantify parameter interactions — a natural future extension listed on the About page.
